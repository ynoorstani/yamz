Who I Am

 

A few fun facts

I love french press coffee, I have a cat named after MacGyver, my favorite number is 22, not a huge fan of IPAs, I play video games like it's my life, I knew what I wanted to do at the age of 13, I do downhill mountain biking, my favorite food is bacon and I broke my left arm twice.

 
 

A look into my life

During the warmer months, I enjoy traveling to some great downhill mountain biking locations or finding a remote lake somewhere new for kayaking. Although the majority of the year, you will find me behind a computer screen. I am a passionate gamer which has had a huge impact on the start of my career, but that's a story for another time. 

Throughout the years I've worked for places that feel the need to constantly burnout their employees with horrible requests and ridiculous timelines. Having been put through some bad experiences in my past, I've also had some great ones that allowed me to work with some amazing people who have played key roles in my career. I have worked very hard to get where I am today, but I'm just as passionate about where my career is leading me. The challenges that I deal with on a day-to-day basis I find meaningful and rewarding. Bottom line: I love what I do and I will do whatever it takes to further my career.

My days working at

Andculture

 

Beer and ping pong

While working at Andculture, I was fortunate enough to be a part of the one of the company's largest startup clients. MilkMoney is an entertainment marketplace that focuses on sponsorships, product placements, bookings, and endorsements. The easiest way to explain its function is to imagine eBay mixed with Kickstarter, but geared towards celebrities. This application was the first of its kind and posed many challenges within design and development. The project ended up being a nine month build and consisted of seven employees of which were 100% allocated. 

 
 

The reason I am so proud of MilkMoney is because initially I had a huge learning curve and I was able to overcome it. I was placed on this project immediately after starting at Andculture and I had no experience with Ruby on Rails, Sass, HAML, Git, or having a project ran in agile. Needless to say, this project has had a large impact on my career and put me on the path of becoming a solid front-end developer with a focus on Rails.
